Q2. What are the chemical properties included in inhibitors search results?

Molecular Descriptors:
- RoF = Lipinski's rule of five (number of violation).
- LogP = Log of Partition Coefficient.
- TPSA = Topological Polar Surface Area.
- HBA = number of Hydrogen Bond Acceptor.
- HBD = number of Hydrogen Bond Donor.
- NRB = Number of Rotatable Bonds.
The average values considered for the descriptors were as follows:
- Have a molecular weight (MW) between 309 and 617 Da (average of 463.3)
- Have a ClogP (calculated with RDKit) between 1.4 and 6.7 (average of 4.0)
- Contain between 0 and 4 hydrogen bond donors (HBD) (average of 2.1)
- Contain between 3 and 11 hydrogen bond acceptors (HBA) (average of 6.7)

Q4. What do the terms SMILES and InCHI key refer to?

1. SMILES (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) is a chemical notation that allows a user to represent a chemical structure in a way that can be used by the computer.
2. The IUPAC InCHI (International Chemical Identifier) is a textual identifier for chemical substances which are designed to provide a standard way to encode molecular information and to facilitate the search for such information in databases and on the web.
3. InChIKey is a fixed-length format directly derived from InChI. It is based on a strong hash (SHA-256 algorithm) of an InChI string.

Q5. What are the chromosome X,chromosome Y & chromosome MT refer to?

The X & Y chromosome are the two sex chromosomes.Females have two X chromosomes, while males have one X and one Y chromosome.
MT chromosome is a small circular chromosome composed of mitochondrial DNA usually found inside mitochondria.

Q6. How to access the NCBI Sqeuence Viewer in the phosphosite search?

1. Select the chromosome you are interested in.
2. Copy the Substrate gene name in the find dialogue box.
3. Double right click on the feature results and click close to view the sequence.
4. Hover on the sequence to find all the general information for the substrate and click on the sequence to find all the isoforms present.

Q7. How is Relative Kinase Activity calculated?

The relative kinase activity barplot shows the activity of each identified kinase in both control and inhibited samples.
1. Relative Kinase Activity = (Sum of control mean's of a kinase)/(Total control mean of the sample)
2. Relative Inhibited Activity = (Sum of inhibitor mean's of a kinase)/(Total inhibitor mean of the sample)
